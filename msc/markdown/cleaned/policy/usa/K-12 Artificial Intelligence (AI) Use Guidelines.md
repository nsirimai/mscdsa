# Table of Contents

## Concept

The Northport-East Northport Union Free School District recognizes that artificial intelligence is reshaping education, offering both opportunities and challenges. Our K-12 AI Guidelines represent a forward-thinking approach to integrating AI technologies in ways that enhance learning while preserving the essential human elements of education. We believe AI should serve as a tool that amplifies, rather than replaces, the expertise and judgment of our educators.

## Background

The rapid evolution of AI technologies, particularly generative AI, has created a pressing need for educational frameworks that address their use in schools. Our district has developed these guidelines in response to this changing landscape, drawing from emerging best practices and research in educational technology. The guidelines reflect our commitment to excellence in education while acknowledging that AI tools, when properly implemented, can support student learning, reduce teacher workload, and prepare students for a technology-rich future.

## Abstract

The Northport-East Northport UFSD AI Guidelines provide a comprehensive framework for integrating artificial intelligence in K-12 education. These guidelines establish clear protocols for AI use across different grade levels, emphasizing teacher expertise, student privacy, and academic integrity. They address key concerns including data security, equity of access, and appropriate use cases while maintaining focus on our core educational mission. The document outlines specific roles and responsibilities for students, teachers, staff, and administrators, providing practical guidance for implementation while ensuring flexibility to adapt to evolving technologies.

## Understanding the Guidelines:

Think of these AI guidelines as a roadmap for our educational community, designed to help everyone navigate the new landscape of artificial intelligence in schools. At their heart, these guidelines are about using AI thoughtfully and responsibly to enhance learning while maintaining the crucial role of human judgment and expertise.

For students, the journey begins in elementary school with carefully supervised exposure to AI tools, progressing through middle school with more independent but guided use, and culminating in high school with increasingly autonomous application for college and career preparation. Throughout this progression, students learn not just how to use AI, but how to use it ethically and effectively.

Teachers remain central to this process as active guides and evaluators. The guidelines emphasize that while AI can support teaching and reduce administrative burden, educators are the ultimate judges of AI-generated content's appropriateness and accuracy. Their professional expertise guides how AI is integrated into the classroom, ensuring it serves educational goals rather than directing them.

The guidelines also address practical concerns about safety and privacy. They specify which AI tools are approved for use (those with proper data privacy agreements) and establish clear boundaries about sharing personal information. The guidelines prioritize protecting our students while helping them learn to be responsible digital citizens.

Accountability is embedded throughout the framework, with clear consequences for misuse but also clear support for proper use. The guidelines aren't meant to restrict but to empower, helping everyone in our educational community understand how to leverage AI effectively while maintaining academic integrity and educational excellence.

Regular review and updates of these guidelines ensure they remain relevant as technology evolves, reflecting our commitment to preparing students for a future where AI will be an increasingly important tool in their educational and professional lives.

## Development of the K-12 AI Use Guidelines: A Collaborative Journey

The development of Northport-East Northport's K-12 AI Use Guidelines represents a comprehensive, community-driven process that spanned over a year. The initiative began in December 2023 with the formation of a District AI Think Tank, bringing together educators, administrators, and technology specialists to explore AI's educational implications.

This thoughtful approach progressed through several key phases:

## Research and Initial Development (Winter-Spring 2024)

The District AI Think Tank conducted extensive research and engaged in deep discussions about AI's role in education. A district-wide staff survey assessed educator readiness and comfort with AI technologies, providing crucial baseline data for guideline development and tool selection.

## Policy Framework Development (Fall 2024)

The District Technology Committee evaluated research findings and analyzed policy examples from other districts, identifying elements most relevant to Northport-East Northport's unique needs. This work culminated in the first formal draft of the guidelines.

## Stakeholder Engagement and Refinement (January-March 2025)

The guidelines underwent multiple rounds of review and refinement through engagement with key stakeholders:

- District Technology Committee conducted initial review and revision
- NEN Administrative Council
- United Teachers of Northport (UTN)
- Academy of Finance Advisory Group provided business community perspective and student feedback
- Northport High School student government offered student viewpoint
- PTA Council contributed parent and community feedback

## Final Review and Adoption (March 2025)

The process culminated with a presentation to the Board of Education for review and adoption, representing the final step in establishing these comprehensive guidelines.

This inclusive development process ensured the guidelines reflect diverse perspectives and needs while establishing a practical framework for AI integration in our educational environment.

## 1. Introduction, Purpose, and Scope

## Introduction:

The Northport-East Northport Union Free School District recognizes the potential impact generative artificial intelligence (AI) may have on our staff, students, and community. As a learning organization that is committed to excellence in all areas without exception, the staff is encouraged to explore generative AI systems to enhance their learning and development and also educate students about these systems, including the uses of, limitations of, and legal and ethical issues associated with such systems. Understanding the benefits and limitations of generative AI is crucial to supporting students as they learn the skills needed to successfully engage as Future-Forward ready members of society within this technological landscape.

These guidelines have been developed to show responsible generative AI usage which addresses data privacy concerns, terms of use, and academic honesty and ensure that the benefits of AI are being harnessed in ways that enhance educational experiences and maintain academic integrity, while safeguarding the welfare and privacy of all students and staff. Given the exponential growth of AI, it is crucial to implement guidelines similar to our code of conduct and other governing and guiding rules to help students and staff understand acceptable use while engaging in the educational application of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies within The Northport-East Northport Union Free School District.

## Purpose:

These guidelines will serve to educate our students, staff, and school communities on the appropriate and responsible use of artificial intelligence (AI), particularly generative AI tools, in classroom instruction, school management, and systemwide operations. Generative AI has potential benefits for education and risks that must be thoughtfully managed. This document serves to;

- Provide clear guidance on AI use across K-12 grade levels.
- Support students' learning while upholding academic integrity.
- Ensure equitable, responsible access to AI technologies.
- Foster ethical technology use and digital citizenship.
- Aid educators in effective AI tool integration.

This guidance complements existing policies on technology use, data protection, academic integrity, and student support.

## 2. Definition of AI Technologies

Artificial intelligence refers to computer systems that are taught to automate tasks normally requiring human intelligence. "Generative AI" refers to tools, such as but not limited to; Claude, Gemini, Bing Chat, ChatGPT, Mid-Journey, and Dall-E, that can produce new content, such as text, images, or music, based on patterns they've learned from their training data. This is made possible through "machine learning," a subset of AI where computers learn from data without being explicitly programmed for a specific task. Think of it as teaching a computer to be creative based on examples it has seen! While generative AI tools show great promise and often make useful suggestions, they are designed to predict what is right, which isn't always right. As a result, their output can be inaccurate, misleading, or incomplete.

AI technologies refer to digital systems capable of performing tasks that generally require human intelligence, including but not limited to:

- Large Language Models (e.g., ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Claude, Bing, Co-Pilot)
- AI-powered educational tools and tutoring systems
- AI image and creative content generators
- AI writing and editing assistants
- AI research and analysis tools

AI-generated content includes any form of digital media-text, images, audio, or video-created by AI without direct human intervention, using advanced algorithms and machine learning techniques.

## 3. General Principles

Our AI use is guided by the following principles:

## 1. Educational Purpose:

The use of generative AI in educational and administrative settings to help us reach our district goals, including improving student learning, teacher effectiveness, and school operations. Our goal is to continue to build infrastructure and capacity to support the use of AI

resources throughout the entire organization and make AI tools equally accessible to all students, teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates.

## 2. Ethical Use:

AI tools will be evaluated for biases and ethical concerns, ensuring they effectively serve our diverse educational community.

## 3. Safety and Privacy:

AI is one of many technologies used in our schools, and its use will align with district policies and regulations to protect student privacy and ensure accessibility to those with disabilities, and protect against harmful content. We will not share personally identifiable information with consumer-based AI systems. We will thoroughly evaluate existing and future technologies in accordance with CIPA, FERPA, and NYS Ed Law 2d for compliance. The district continues to utilize internal and external systems for monitoring and filtering inappropriate use of technology or technology tools.

## 4. Transparency:

Promoting AI literacy among students and staff is central to addressing the risks of AI use, teaches critical skills for students' futures, and prepares students to be responsible and ethical digital citizens. Students and staff will be given support to develop their AI literacy, which includes how to use AI, when to use it, and how it works. We will support teachers in adapting instruction in a context where all students have access to generative AI tools.

## 5. Alignment with The Profile of a Graduate:

AI should support the competencies anchored in the district profile of a graduate including the attributes that would be observed in Critical Thinkers, Effective Communicators, Innovative Problem Solvers, Global Citizens, and Collaborators.

## 6. Safety First:

The district will work to realize the benefits of AI in education, address risks associated with using AI, and evaluate if and when to use AI tools, paying special attention to misinformation and bias.

## 7. Academic Integrity:

Users must respect intellectual property rights and consider the ethical implications of AI usage, including issues of bias and misinformation. Honesty, trust, fairness, respect, and responsibility continue to be expectations for both students and teachers. Students should be truthful in giving credit to sources and tools and honest in presenting work that is genuinely their own for evaluation and feedback.

## 8. Student and Teacher Agency:

AI tools can provide recommendations or enhance decision-making, but staff and students should be 'critical consumers' when using AI to inform any organizational and academic decisions and changes. Students, teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates will be responsible and accountable for pedagogical or decision-making processes where AI systems may inform decision-making.

## 9. Continued Monitoring and Review of AI Guidelines:

Understanding that AI and technologies are evolving rapidly, we commit to frequent and regular reviews and updates of our policies, procedures, and practices.

## 4. Grade-Level Appropriate Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI)

## General - All Grades (K-12)

## Students

- The only Generative AI systems NENUFSD students are allowed to use are those available directly or as part of other software listed in the NENUFSD Application List.
- A complete list of approved software with Student Data Privacy Agreements can be found at https://portal.classlink.com/northport:
- There are approved AI systems (such as Canva, Magic School AI, Brisk, Gemini) that have signed Student Data Privacy Agreements. Therefore, students may use AI features embedded within these tools with permission and proper citation.
- Assistance from an AI system is not allowed when it interferes with the educational objectives or the assessment of a submitted work. The use of generative AI to complete one's work can also be forbidden at the discretion of the student's teacher.
- Students who use any generative AI systems must do so within the context of teacher guidance and direction, including proper citation, or they may face consequences under the code of conduct.

## Teachers, Staff, Administrators, and Affiliates

- Teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates are encouraged to explore Generative AI systems for themselves, and to teach their students about such systems, including their uses, limitations, and legal and ethical issues associated with such systems. These tools can assist teachers in their work, and an understanding of these systems is crucial to supporting students as they learn to work in an evolving social and technology environment.

- If Teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates take the initiative to use generative AI systems, they must comply with all the K-12 Generative AI Guidelines.

## Elementary School (K-5)

- Introduction to AI use with guidance and supervision while in school.
- Emphasis on understanding basic AI concepts and digital citizenship.
- Restricted to educational games and interactive learning under parental guidance.
- Parents/guardians must oversee any at-home AI use.

## Middle School (6-8)

- Introduction to independent AI use with teacher guidance.
- Focus on responsible use, including evaluating AI-generated content.
- Students should learn to properly attribute AI assistance.
- Integration during supervised classroom activities, emphasizing its role as a learning aid.
- Parents/guardians must oversee any at-home AI use.

## High School (9-12)

- More autonomous use of AI tools with appropriate oversight for college and career readiness.
- Focus on developing advanced AI literacy skills.
- Support for research and complex problem-solving where AI can enhance learning.

## 5. Ethical and Responsible Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI)

## General (K-12)

The district recognizes that responsible uses of AI will vary depending on the context, such as a classroom activity or assignment. Teachers will clarify if, when, and how AI tools will be used, with input from students and families, while the school system will ensure compliance with applicable laws and regulations regarding data security and privacy. Appropriate AI use should be guided by the specific parameters and objectives defined for an activity. Below are some examples of responsible uses that serve educational goals.

## Practices to Follow:

## Student Learning

- Aiding Creativity: Students can harness generative AI to generate creativity across diverse subjects and content areas.
- Collaboration: Generative AI tools can partner with students in group projects by contributing concepts, supplying research support, and identifying relationships between varied information.

- Communication: AI can offer students real-time translation, personalized language exercises, and interactive dialogue simulations.
- Content Creation and Enhancement: AI can help generate personalized study materials, summaries, quizzes, and visual aids, help students organize thoughts and content, and help review content.
- Tutoring: AI technologies have the potential to provide opportunities for one-to-one tutoring and support, making personalized learning more accessible for all students. AI-powered virtual teaching assistants may provide continuous support, answer questions, help with homework, and supplement classroom instruction.
- Accessibility: AI technologies can provide access to instructional materials, curriculum, and methods of assessment for students with disabilities. Considerations may be appropriate to remove barriers of a disability and to provide a means to measure student comprehension of skills and content.

## Teacher Support

- Assessment Design, Analysis and Student Feedback: In addition to enhancing assessment design by creating questions and providing standardized feedback on common mistakes, AI can conduct diagnostic assessments to identify gaps in knowledge or skills and inform the teachers instructional decisions for supporting students. Teachers will ultimately be responsible for evaluation, feedback, and grading, including determining and assessing the usefulness of AI in supporting their grading work. AI will not be solely responsible for grading. AI can support teachers in building efficiencies for providing students with real-time actionable feedback on their tasks, assignments, projects, and assessments.
- Content Development, Personalized Learning and Enhancement for Differentiation: AI can assist educators by differentiating curricula, suggesting lesson plans, generating diagrams and charts, and customizing independent practice based on student needs and proficiency levels. AI can assist teachers with increasing student voice and choice through individualized and personalized learning experiences. Personalized study materials, study guides, visual aides and accommodations for students with disabilities.
- Continuous Professional Development: AI can guide educators by recommending teaching and learning strategies based on student needs, personalizing professional development to teachers' needs and interests, suggesting collaborative projects between subjects or teachers, and offering simulation-based training scenarios such as teaching a lesson or managing a parent/teacher conference.
- Research and Resource Compilation: AI can help educators by recommending books or articles relevant to a lesson and updating teachers on teaching techniques, research, and methods.

## School Management and Operations

- Communications: AI tools can help draft and refine communications within the school community, deploy chatbots for routine inquiries, and provide instant language translation.

- Operational Efficiency: Staff can use AI tools to support school operations and streamline administrative processes, including scheduling courses, automating inventory management, increasing energy savings, and generating performance reports.
- Learning Management Systems (LMS) : AI can analyze student performance data to provide insights to educators, helping them tailor instruction or interventions. An example of this is the district's use of its data warehouse eduCLIMBER.

## Actions to Avoid:

## Student Learning

- Bullying/harassment: Using AI tools to manipulate media to impersonate others for bullying, harassment, or any form of intimidation is strictly prohibited. All users are expected to employ these tools solely for educational purposes, upholding values of respect, inclusivity, and academic integrity at all times.
- Overreliance: Dependence on AI tools can decrease human discretion and oversight. Important nuances and context can be overlooked and accepted. Teachers will clarify if, when, and how AI tools should be used in their classrooms, and teachers and students are expected to review outputs generated by AI before use.
- Plagiarism and cheating: Students and staff should not copy from any source, including generative AI, without prior approval and adequate documentation. Students should not submit AI-generated work as their original work. Staff and students will be taught how to properly cite or acknowledge the use of AI where applicable. Teachers will be clear about when and how AI tools may be used to complete assignments and restructure assignments to reduce opportunities for plagiarism by requiring personal context, original arguments, or original data collection. Existing procedures related to potential violations of our Plagiarism Policy must be followed in accordance with the Code of Conduct.
- Unequal access: If an assignment permits the use of AI tools, the tools will be made available to all students, considering that some may already have access to such resources outside of school.

## Teacher Support

- Societal Bias: AI tools trained on human data will inherently reflect societal biases in the data. Risks include reinforcing stereotypes, recommending inappropriate educational interventions, or making discriminatory evaluations, such as falsely reporting plagiarism by non-native English speakers. Staff and students will be taught to understand the origin and implications of societal bias in AI, AI tools will be evaluated for the diversity of their training data and transparency, and humans will review all AI-generated outputs before use.
- Diminishing student and teacher agency and accountability: While generative AI can support teaching and reduce workload, educators remain the authoritative judges of AI-generated content based on their subject expertise. Teachers will use their professional knowledge to evaluate AI outputs for accuracy and appropriateness, ensuring AI serves as a tool to augment rather than replace human judgment. The responsibility for instruction, mentoring, assessment and student development stays firmly with educators, who must

critically assess any AI-generated content through the lens of their professional expertise before classroom use.

## S chool Management and Operations

- Compromising Privacy: The education system will not use AI in ways that compromise teacher or student privacy or lead to unauthorized data collection, as this violates privacy laws and our system's ethical principles.
- Noncompliance with Existing Policies: We will evaluate AI tools for compliance with all relevant policies and regulations, such as privacy laws and ethical principles. AI tools will be required to detail if/how personal information is used to ensure that personal data remains confidential and isn't misused.

## 6. Academic Integrity

Students, teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates will use AI to advance the fundamental values of academic integrity - honesty, trust, fairness, respect, and responsibility.

- Students, teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates can use AI tools to fact-check by quickly cross-referencing information and claims, though they must still be critical of the output.
- Advanced AI tools can increase fairness by identifying and minimizing biases in grading and assessments.
- AI can adapt materials for students with different learning needs, showing respect for individual differences.
- Students, teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates might allow the limited use of generative AI on specific assignments or parts of assignments and articulate why they do not allow its use in other assignments.
- Students, teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates may use technologies designed to identify the use of generative AI to detect cheating and plagiarism. In cases where AI tools are used to evaluate for originality and authenticity or detect cheating or plagiarism, the teacher will have the authority to make the final judgement as to such determinations.
- When students, teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates use an AI system, its use must be disclosed and explained through the appropriate form of citation.

## 7. Digital Citizenship

Students and educators must:

- Respect intellectual property rights and validate information from multiple sources.
- Protect personal and private data while using AI.
- Report any misuse or inappropriate content encountered.
- Contribute positively to the digital learning environment.

## 8. Equity and Inclusion

In order to ensure equitable access to AI technologies:

- The district will provide necessary resources to support students.
- Students without home access will receive appropriate support.
- AI tools will be selected with accessibility in mind to benefit all students, including those with diverse learning needs.
- Regular assessments will be conducted to address access and usage patterns.

## 9. Security, Privacy, and Safety

The district is committed to providing comprehensive support to ensure the appropriate level of security, privacy, and safety for Students, teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates using AI. We will implement reasonable security measures to secure AI technologies against unauthorized access and misuse. All AI systems deployed within the school will be evaluated for compliance with relevant laws and regulations, including those related to data protection, privacy, and students' online safety.

Students, teachers, staff, administrators, and affiliates are prohibited from entering confidential or personally identifiable information into unauthorized AI tools, such as those without approved data privacy agreements. Sharing confidential or personal data with an AI system could violate privacy if not properly disclosed and consented to.

Professional Development: Educators will receive ongoing training and resources to effectively integrate AI tools into their teaching practices. This includes workshops, seminars, and access to instructional materials.

Parent/Guardian Education: Educational sessions will be available for parents and guardians to understand the role of AI in education and how to support their children in using these technologies responsibly.

Monitoring and Evaluation: The use of AI technologies will be regularly monitored to evaluate their impact on learning and teaching. Feedback will be collected from users to improve implementation strategies.

## 10. Violations and Consequences

It is important to adhere to these guidelines to maintain a safe and ethical learning environment. Violations will be addressed according to the district's code of conduct and technology acceptable use policies.

- Assessment of Violations: Any occurrence of guideline violations, such as unauthorized use of AI for assignments or failure to properly attribute AI content, will be evaluated by educational staff.
- Consequences: Depending on the severity and nature of the violation, consequences may include:

- Loss of AI tool privileges for a specified period.
- Academic penalties, such as reduced grades or assignment re-evaluation.
- Mandatory additional training sessions on responsible AI use.
- Notification of parents/guardians and potential involvement in resolution.
- Disciplinary action aligned with existing school policies.

## 11. Agreement and Acknowledgment

To ensure thorough understanding and compliance with these guidelines:

- Annual Review: All students, parents, and guardians are required to review the AI guidelines annually to stay informed about any updates or changes.
- Acknowledgment Form: A signed acknowledgment form must be submitted by students and their parents/guardians, confirming their understanding of the guidelines and acceptance of responsibility for appropriate AI use.
- Partnership with Families: Parents and guardians agree to actively monitor their children's use of AI at home and collaborate with educators to promote responsible and ethical use of technology.
- Open Communication: Families are encouraged to communicate any concerns or questions regarding AI use to appropriate school staff, ensuring a cooperative approach to integrating AI in education.
