## Artificial Intelligence (AI) Policy


## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to provide a framework for the safe and responsible use of Artificial Intelligence tools and technologies at Alkira Secondary College. It seeks to provide students and members of the school community an understanding that:

- This policy is an extension to the existing Digital Learning policy which all parents/students sign
- Recognition of emerging technologies and their use in an educational environment
- The prioritisation of academic integrity across the college to support key authentication requirements of student work in line with the VCAA
- Ongoing commitment to educational equity

## Scope

This policy applies to all students and staff at Alkira Secondary College, inclusive of the College's Student Acceptable Use policy.

Staff use of technology is also governed by the following Department policies which outline the safe and supportive use of technology in an educational context:

- [Acceptable Use Policy for ICT Resources](https://www2.education.vic.gov.au/pal/ict-acceptable-use/overview)
- [Cybersafety and Responsible Use of Digital Technologies](https://www2.education.vic.gov.au/pal/digital-technologies-responsible-use/policy)
- [Generative Artificial Intelligence](https://www2.education.vic.gov.au/pal/generative-artificial-intelligence/policy)
- [Digital Learning in Schools](https://www2.education.vic.gov.au/pal/digital-learning/policy)

Staff, volunteers and school councillors also need to adhere to codes of conduct relevant to their respective roles. These codes include:

- Alkira Secondary College's Child Safety Code of Conduct
- [The Victorian Teaching Profession Code of Conduct (teaching staff)](https://www.vit.vic.edu.au/)
- [Code of Conduct for Victorian Sector Employees (staff)](https://www2.education.vic.gov.au/pal/code-conduct/overview)
- [Code of Conduct for Directors of Victorian Public Entities (school councillors)](https://www2.education.vic.gov.au/pal/school-council-conduct/policy)

## Definitions

For the purpose of this policy, 'Artificial Intelligence (AI)' refers to current and future generative AI technologies including but not restricted to Large Language Model (LLM), image generation, audio, video, and multi-modal technologies. These tools, platforms, software, online and offline applications of AI are accessible to students and teachers for educational purposes, and their use is subject to the guidelines and restrictions outlined in this policy.

## Policy

## General Statement

Artificial Intelligence is a rapidly changing and relatively new area entering into education, and as such Alkira Secondary College recognises this development and the impact that AI may have in the near future for the school and the broader community/workforce. Outlining a broad policy of acceptable use conditions for both staff and students, this policy provides the flexibility to adapt to AI technologies as they become more integrated into the school.

As a partial extension to the Digital Learning policy, this policy abides by pre-existing conditions, curriculum and requirements outlined therein.

In circumstances where students are required to access AI tools and technologies which require any personal information to be provided, there will be appropriate communication to parents/carers to meet legislative requirements on privacy and equity. Communication may be provided through Compass or physical letter.

The use of AI within the school will be monitored on an ongoing basis for both staff and students and this policy will be regularly reviewed and updated.

## Staff

## Ethics

As outlined in the Victorian Education policy on Generative Artificial Intelligence, staff are required to act ethically with respect to use of AI. In particular the use of AI in communication, marking, providing feedback and reporting. This includes not using AI tools to directly:

- Communicate with parents/carers or students
- Make judgements about student learning achievement or progress
- Write student feedback or reports for parents/carers

AI tools can be used to contribute to those activities, but AI tools are not a replacement for proof reading and human input.

## Staff should aim to:

- Model safe and responsible use of AI by following this policy and associated guidance
- Be aware of limitations, potential biases of AI technology
- Be aware of how AI functions and the potential opportunities it can provide
- Stay up to date through College and Department of Education Professional Learning

## Privacy

Staff should not use any identifiable information of staff, students or members of the school community during the use of AI tools. This includes but not limited to text, images, audio and video. Staff should use their professional judgement when using tools and entering information around assessment results, attendance, school, etc.

As many of these tools/platforms are produced by companies who utilise user data for future AI training, inclusion of personal data may be reused by the platform/company and could lead to an accidental privacy breach.

## Academic Integrity/Integrity in General

AI tools represent a large risk to academic integrity in a school setting. As AI tools become more ubiquitous staff are advised to manage and maintain academic integrity in a number of ways.

- Setting expectations around AI use in the classroom by following recommendations and College guidance on the topic
- Designing assessments with consideration of AI tools
- Identifying and responding to misuse of AI tools by students as soon as possible through identified processes (staff to see AI guidance documentation for more detail)
- Disclose AI use to students where appropriate as part of modelling responsible use (examples may include, generated text samples or step-by-step instructions generated by AI)

## Students

## Academic Integrity

In line with Alkira Secondary College values, Integrity generally and Academic Integrity specifically are essential to developing student academic success and development. The use of AI tools and technologies has provided opportunities for students to access knowledge in ways that can be both helpful and harmful to learning. Students are encouraged to only utilise AI in ways that are approved by the college generally and teacher specifically.

This means that while students may be able to and encouraged to access certain tools, they are not to misuse those tools by:

- Using an AI to write their work for them (plagiarism)
- Answering questions for them (cheating)
- Invalidate an assessment task through AI use when instructed not to

Students should be aware of the inherent issues and limitations of AI use particularly around specific facts, details, dates, knowledge and biases.

## Responsible Use

Responsible use of AI for students primarily refers to the safe use and access to AI tools and technologies, avoiding entering any personal information, identifiable data or similar. Students must not:

- Upload media containing depictions of themselves, other students, staff, parents/carers or members of the school community (including text, images/photos, audio and video).
- Generate media which depicts the likeness of students, staff, parents/carers or members of the school community (including text, images, audio and video)
- Generate artefacts that mimic a cultural tradition in a way that is disrespectful or offensive (for example, images mimicking indigenous artwork)

If students are unsure whether their AI use represents responsible use, they should seek additional advice from teaching staff.

Responsible use of AI at Alkira Secondary College will predominantly be related to academic activities as directed by teachers.

## Disclosure

When advised, students are required to identify their use of AI tools in connection to specific assessment tasks. There may be occasions where AI may be:

- Encouraged
- Recommended
- Required
- Restricted
- Prohibited

Students will be made aware of these conditions in advance and the requirements around disclosing their use. This will be reiterated and enforced for assessed coursework tasks.

This may include a statement as part of an assessment task that AI was used in 'xyz' ways, or it may require a more in-depth identification of use.

## Misuse and Consequences

As AI tools and technologies are an emerging area in education and at Alkira Secondary College the misuse and consequences of misuse will adapt accordingly.  General misuse is to be treated similarly to existing ICT misuse procedures. More specific misuse in a classroom setting can be treated appropriately by the classroom teacher, but may include:

- Teacher managed consequences
- Assessment task invalidation
- Communication to student parent/carer

More serious misuse of AI technology that breaches the privacy or safety of students, staff, parents/carers or members of the school community will be treated on a case-by-case basis and may lead to police involvement, dependent on the severity.
