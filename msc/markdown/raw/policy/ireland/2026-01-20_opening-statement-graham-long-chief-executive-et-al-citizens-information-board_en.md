

## Citizens Information Board Opening Statement to the Oireachtas Joint Committee on Artificial Intelligence

## Meeting of 20 January 2026

Good morning Chair, Deputies, Senators, On behalf of CIB, thank you for the invitation to address the Committee on the role and impact of AI in the public sector. This is a topic that is shaping the future of how we deliver services and engage with members of the public. The Citizens Information Board celebrated its 25th anniversary last June, while the Citizens Information Services that we fund and support celebrated their 50th anniversary in 2025. As a State agency, CIB is supported by the Department of Social Protection. Our vision is an Ireland where everyone has access to a trusted, independent source of information, advice and advocacy . This remit is not just a function, it is a cornerstone of democracy and social inclusion. Our work spans critical areas such as information on social welfare entitlements, employment rights and health services. CIB's reach is extensive and is very much rooted in communities. We fund and support a network of vital services. Alongside the Citizens Information Services (CIS), there is the Money Advice and Budgeting Service (MABS), the National Advocacy Service for People with Disabilities (NAS), the Sign Language Interpreting Service (SLIS) and the Register of Irish Sign Language Interpreters. These organisations can be lifelines for people facing challenges, making decisions, and seeking clarity in moments that matter. To give you a sense of the level of activity, the Citizens Information Services assisted more than 384,000 people last year, with just under 709,000 queries. The Citizens Information Phone Service answered more than 111,000 calls. CIB runs the citizensinformation.ie website directly. Last year the website welcomed more than 8.6 million users and recorded more than 27.4 million pageviews. CIB plays a pivotal role in providing

independent, trusted information.



As we developed our current digital strategy, one message was very clear, which is that choice matters. People want the convenience of online services but not at the expense of face-to-face support. Our Board is committed to preserving that human connection while embracing new and improved technologies. Our digital strategy reflects this balance, seeking to enhance services without losing the empathy and trust that define our work. One element of this is collaborating on an initiative from the Citizens Information Services to launch a Digital Help Service, designed to help users access online public services confidently, while building digital literacy. However, we do face new challenges, particularly as AI tools become integrated into people's digital lives. Alongside the use of AI to generate online content, the rapid rise of tools such as Google's AI Overviews has already impacted traffic to citizensinformation.ie, with an almost 19% decline last year. These tools often extract and mix CIB's content with other sources, some of which may be outdated or inaccurate. In response, we have been working with the Office of the Government Chief Information Officer to develop an AI tool called AskCI. At the end of last month we set AskCI live for users for a 'soft launch'. This followed the establishment of a strong governance framework and a considerable amount of assessment and testing, including with the Citizens Information Services. Unlike generic AI systems, AskCI draws only from our verified content on citizensinformation.ie. It uses natural language processing to deliver clear, conversational answers, acting as a translator of trusted information. AskCI works in multiple languages and helps to make information more accessible for our users. As of 13 January, AskCI had recorded 1,345 sessions and 2,922 questions. Areas of interest for users are broadly in line with the website, covering social welfare, money and tax, employment, moving country and housing. CIB's Digital Content team is monitoring the accuracy and appropriateness of the responses generated by AskCI. We will be tracking AskCI over the next few months to see how it gains traction with users and how it sits alongside our website content. Future options for AskCI include a voice interface, the use of a

pop-up overlay on the website, or re-purposing it as an app.



While the early results are encouraging, AskCI is simply a new way to explore our website content. It is not a substitute for in-depth research or for in-person and personalised information, advice or advocacy. The AI landscape for us is complex, with AI both a powerful way to generate misinformation and a

powerful tool to interact with information generally. Like most organisations, we will be watching developments in this area very closely over the coming months and years.
