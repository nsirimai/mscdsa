## Opening Statement of Ruth Kennedy, Revenue Commissioner, to the Joint Committee on Artificial Intelligence on 20 January 2026.

Thank you, Cathaoirleach, for the opportunity to make an opening statement on behalf of Revenue. I understand today's meeting is to focus on Revenue's use of Artificial Intelligence.

I am accompanied today by my colleague Keith Redmond, Revenue's Chief Technology Officer.

Revenue's mission is to serve the community by fairly and efficiently collecting taxes and duties and implementing customs controls. Its vision is to be a leading tax and customs administration, trusted by the community and an employer of choice.

Revenue has a long history of using technology and innovation to support the delivery of its mission and vision. As far back as 1963, Revenue purchased the first modern computer in the Irish Civil Service. This was the second computer ever installed in an Irish organisation.

Over 25 years ago, Revenue 's Online Service, ROS, was launched which allowed taxpayers file returns and make payments online. Since then, Revenue has introduced a wide range of digital services from myAccount to electronic customs declarations and real time payroll reporting. Last year, over 87 million transactions were processed by Revenue's digital services. The availability of these services reduces the administrative burden on taxpayers and makes it as easy as possible to be tax compliant.

Artificial Intelligence or AI is frequently considered a new technology. While the new tools now available no doubt provide greater speed, scale and flexibility, the use of AI is well established in Revenue and in tax and customs administrations globally. While Revenue does not use AI to make tax-related decisions or carry out its statutory functions, its use is very much part of what Revenue does and how it operates.

For many years Revenue has leveraged such technologies to assess tax risk and detect potential non-compliant behaviours. This enables Revenue to allocate compliance resources more effectively. Revenue 's PAYE real-time risk system, which uses a combination of predictive analytics and rules-based models to detect risk in PAYE refund claims, received a Taoiseach's award in 2012.

While Revenue has been using AI for a long time, technological advances over the last few years, particularly in respect of Large Language Models, means that the current focus is on the application of AI to deliver improvements to both external facing service provided to taxpayers and their agents as well as internal facing operational efficiency.

For example, Revenue has deployed AI models to assist in more accurately directing taxpayer queries to the appropriate Revenue caseworker, assist staff in accessing all relevant information to allow them promptly and accurately respond to taxpayer queries and generate initial drafts of Tax and Duty Manuals which are then rigorously verified and refined by subject matter experts before publication. The commonality across these models is that they are designed to support human decision making with a clear focus on gaining efficiencies. More information on these AI solutions is included in the briefing provided to the Committee.

Revenue owns and manages a very large and highly integrated set of ICT services. Revenue has long established, embedded and robust governance arrangements in place to oversee their development and deployment, and to ensure value for money is being achieved in relation to the associated expenditure.

Revenue's IT Executive has oversight of all ICT spend and each system development project has a tailored governance structure supporting its delivery. This approach ensures risks are managed and mitigated, and that project objectives and benefits are realised within budget and delivery timelines.

Additional governance arrangements are in place in respect of the development and deployment of AI solutions, including that developments are in line with both the EU AI Act and the national guidelines for the responsible use of AI in the Public Sector. Revenue 's effectiveness as a tax and customs administration depends on the public's trust and confidence in its fairness and integrity. As such, robust internal processes are in place that prioritise ethical standards, data protection, and meaningful human oversight in the design of all AI solutions.

To conclude , Revenue's approach to leveraging technology, including AI, is a direct continuation of a multi-decade long strategy to invest in ICT in a manner that aligns with its corporate priorities and enables the provision of quality digital services . This ensures that Revenue's critical core business is maintained in a way that adds value and drives efficiencies.

The development of transformative and innovative business solutions, some of which are AI led, support Revenue in fulfilling its mission to better serve the community. Every innovation is carefully adopted and governed, ensuring Revenue remains a leading, secure, and modern administration for the years to come.

My colleague and I are happy to answer any questions the Committee may have in relation to Revenue's use of AI during today's meeting. Thank you
