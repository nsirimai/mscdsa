There is limited understanding of what AI means in theoretical and practical terms in the European youth sector. This study revealed that there is a need for a more unified and accessible definition of AI that is specifically contextualised for the youth sector's needs. Such a definition could allow for a more strategic examination of how different aspects of Artificial Intelligence have influenced the sector, if and how their presence and impact might be recognised in the future. To achieve this, it might be useful to build upon the existing AI youth-driven studies, available AI literacy frameworks, and the outcomes of this study and to further research, map, and categorise the different ways in which AI can be seen in the sector. Only by learning what the shared experiences are in the sector, can the sector establish the baseline for ethical and practice standards for AI in youth work. It is important to note that any attempts to co-create AI meaning and understanding in the sector should be grounded in the youth sector values and with the understanding that it should be agile, flexible, and open-ended to continue to respond to any changes associated with the automatisation of the sector.
